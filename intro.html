Defective Imperfect Flawed Nostalgic

Warm colors. Soft lines. Raw exposure. Low resolution. That is the root of 21st-century nostalgic digital media. Photos and videos that capture the roughness of an image or poor quality can take people back in time to a point where technology had less expectaion. Without us even noticing, time moves forward, and so does technology. What new generations don’t understand is that before TVs accurately depicted life in a two-dimensional format and phones could be zoomed in to see detail on the moon, there was vibrance and grain.

Inapplicable to Aesthetic

Features that defined digital media in the early 2000s, such as low resolution, have shifted from practical limitations to aesthetic virtues. People who lived through this phase in technology reminisce in this style or question whether these are even “good” photos or videos. On the other hand, people who did not experience digital media at that time have wanted to capture this aesthetic. By altering media and embracing visual noise and imperfection, they have been able to create a portal into the past.
