Quality Over Quality

Digital compression is a compromise. Compressing an image achieves smaller file sizes to save storage space, lower bandwidth usage, and speed up loading time. Compression allows artists to reduce file size, but at the cost of “quality”. It achieves smaller file sizes by discarding vital information for reduction. Vital in the sense that the image will no longer appear the same. The choice depends on perspective. It all depends on what is more important to the creator and the product's audience: qualitity of the convenience or quality of the clarity.

It's still art.

Art is the subjective expression of ideas and emotions through creative skill and/or imagination. The keyword is subjective. While there’s a general understanding of what art is, it has no single definition and can take many different forms or no form at all. So, if compressing an image means compromising certain aspects of the artwork, it does not mean the art is "compromised". Whether a compressed product was intentional or a result of compromise, it's still art. Through compression, images gain a whole new aspect to reflect on and may even add to the value of the work.
